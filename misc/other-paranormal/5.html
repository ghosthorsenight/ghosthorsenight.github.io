so, a couple of friends and i finally worked up the nerve to visit this old abandoned asylum that’s been rumored to be haunted for years. we’d been talking about it for a while, and we figured it would be a fun, creepy adventure. you know, the classic “let’s scare ourselves silly” kind of thing. but honestly, it turned out way scarier than we expected.

the asylum is way out in the woods, and even just walking up to it gave me chills. it’s huge—like, four stories of broken windows, peeling paint, and a super eerie vibe. we went late at night, of course, because why not make it extra terrifying? we brought flashlights and a camera, hoping to catch some paranormal stuff, but none of us were really prepared for what happened.

as soon as we stepped inside, it felt off. the air was heavy, and it was dead silent except for the occasional creak from the old building settling. we started exploring, walking down long, empty hallways lined with old patient rooms. some doors were still on their hinges, but most were either hanging off or completely missing. i kept feeling like someone was watching us, but whenever i turned around, there was nothing.

about an hour in, we were on the third floor, near what used to be the infirmary. that’s when we heard it—a loud bang, like a door slamming shut. we all froze, shining our flashlights around, but couldn’t see anything. then, we heard footsteps, slow and deliberate, coming from the hallway behind us. at first, we thought it might be someone messing with us, but when we called out, there was no answer. just the sound of those footsteps getting closer. we booked it down the hall, but the footsteps followed us.

we ended up in what looked like an old common area, trying to catch our breath. while we were standing there, my friend suddenly freaked out—she said she saw a shadowy figure standing in the corner of the room, just staring at us. none of us could see it, but she was legit panicking, saying it was moving toward us. we got out of there as fast as we could and didn’t stop running until we were back outside.

honestly, it was the most intense experience i’ve ever had. none of us could explain what happened, but we all agreed that the place is definitely not just an abandoned building. something is in there, and it doesn’t want visitors.
